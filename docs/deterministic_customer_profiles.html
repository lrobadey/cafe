Cafe simulation

Deterministic customer profiles

Customers are not LLM agents.

They are reproducible demand patterns. Emergence comes from profile parameters meeting live cafe state: menu, stock, queue, wait time, seats, and order outcomes.

How one customer is born

The system separates identity, preferences, and behavior so the same seed can produce the same pattern again.

1. Seed

CUSTOMER_RANDOM_SEED

Optional config value creates a repeatable random stream for customer generation.

2. RNG

Shared customer random

Each spawn consumes that stream, then creates a separate visit RNG for top-three order choice and reorder checks.

3. Archetype

Weighted base pattern

The profile starts as a Hurried Commuter, Remote Worker, or Leisure Customer.

4. Profile

Rolled traits

Budget, patience, preferences, dwell time, and order limits are rolled inside archetype ranges.

5. Visit

Deterministic loop

The customer reads live world state, evaluates friction, orders, waits, consumes, maybe reorders, then leaves.

The data streams that create emergence

No one stream is complex. The interesting behavior comes from several small streams meeting every few seconds.

Profile stream

Archetype weight, item preferences, category preferences, dislikes, budget, max items, max orders, patience, seat need, dwell target, reorder chance, and leave-after-pickup.

Menu stream

Only live orderable items are considered. Price filters candidates against remaining budget; item/category scores rank the affordable combinations.

Operations stream

Queue length, elapsed wait, empty table count, and failed stock preparation continually feed the customer friction calculation.

Runtime stream

Orders placed, budget spent, active order, held items, consumed items, table assignment, visit phase, and next reorder time update as the visit unfolds.

Audit map: where the customer brain lives

The “brain” is not one black box. It is a small chain of modules, each owning one kind of decision.

customers/archetypes.py

Defines the behavioral templates: segment weights, taste weights, budget/patience ranges, seating pressure, dwell ranges, reorder chance, and friction constants.

customers/profile.py

Turns one spawn RNG into a frozen profile: picks the weighted archetype, shuffles/ranks preferences, and rolls numeric traits inside archetype ranges.

customers/decisions.py

Calculates the actual choices: friction totals, leave reasons, affordable candidate orders, order scores, top-three selection, and reorder eligibility.

customers/deterministic.py

Runs the visit state machine: enter, friction check, order, seat attempt, wait loop, consume, dwell, reorder, leave, and sync runtime fields.

customers/factory.py

Creates the shared seeded customer RNG, generates the profile, forks a visit RNG, and starts the deterministic visit task.

world.py

Owns reality: menu availability, stock, prices, queue length, table seats, orders, delivery, consumption, customer visit records, and reports.

What gets rolled

  • Archetype is weighted at 35 commuter, 25 worker, and 40 leisure.
  • Preferences include ranked item likes, ranked category likes, and disliked items.
  • Buying limits include budget, max items per order, max orders per visit, and budget already spent.
  • Friction traits include patience, queue sensitivity, no-seat sensitivity, and seat need.
  • Stay behavior includes dwell seconds, reorder chance, reorder check delay, requires-seat-to-dwell, and leave-after-pickup.

What the loop does

  • Order scoring gives preferred items up to 30 points, preferred categories up to 18, disliked items -35, and impossible items -100.
  • Candidate choice builds affordable one- or two-item combinations, sorts by score, then visit RNG picks among the top three.
  • Friction math is queue length times 3/6/10, wait seconds times 0.10/0.20/0.35, plus no-seat penalty 0/8/18/35 and stockout penalty 12.
  • Leaving happens when total friction is greater than patience, or when budget/menu/seating rules make the visit impossible.
  • Reordering requires no active order, remaining budget, available candidates, enough time elapsed, friction within patience, and a reorder-chance roll.

Specific decision calculations

This is the audit layer: the inputs, gates, formulas, and outputs that decide what a customer does.

1. Profile generation

Decision Calculation Output field
Pick segment Roll uniformly from total weight 100: commuter 35, worker 25, leisure 40. archetype_id, display_name
Rank tastes Shuffle item/category keys, then sort by archetype weight descending. This gives stable priority with seeded tie variation. preferred_items, disliked_items, preferred_categories
Roll resources Budget uses uniform(min,max), rounded to cents. Patience, dwell, max items, and reorder delay use integer range rolls. budget, patience, dwell_seconds, max_items_per_order
Copy rules Some behavior is copied directly from archetype, not rolled. max_orders_per_visit, seat_need, queue_sensitivity, no_seat_sensitivity, reorder_chance

2. Order selection

score = item preference + category preference - disliked penalty - budget pressure + multi-item bonus
Stage Exact rule Why it matters
Candidate filter Keep menu items where orderable is true and price is within remaining budget. Live stock and operator menu toggles directly shape demand.
Combination builder Build every 1-item to max_items_per_order combination whose total price fits budget. Workers and leisure customers can produce two-item orders; commuters cannot.
Preference score Preferred item adds 30 - rank*4. Preferred category adds 18 - rank*4. Disliked item subtracts 35. A customer may still pick a lower-ranked item if budget, category, or availability shifts the top options.
Price pressure Subtract (total / budget_remaining) * 8. Two-item orders add 4. Customers prefer good fits, but spending most of the remaining budget slightly hurts a candidate.
Final pick Sort candidates, keep the top three above -50, then visit RNG chooses one. The best-scoring order is not always picked, but the result stays reproducible for a seed.

3. Friction and leaving

total friction = queue + wait + no_seat + stockout
Part Exact calculation Used when
Queue queue_length * QUEUE_WEIGHTS[sensitivity], where low/medium/high are 3/6/10. Before ordering, while waiting, and while dwelling.
Wait elapsed_wait_seconds * WAIT_WEIGHTS[sensitivity], where low/medium/high are 0.10/0.20/0.35. Uses arrival time for general checks, and order placed time while waiting on an active order.
No seat If no open seats and seat need is medium/high, add no-seat sensitivity penalty: none/low/medium/high = 0/8/18/35. Seat pressure can make workers leave even when the queue is manageable.
Stockout After a failed order, add stockout_disappointment * 12. Failed preparation can push a customer over patience or trigger replacement ordering.
Leave gate If total friction > profile.patience, leave. Reason is no seats if no-seat dominates queue, stockout if stockout exists, otherwise impatient. This is the core patience threshold for the deterministic brain.

4. Visit state machine

Phase Gate State written back
arrived Register visit, then immediately test current friction. Profile identity, budget, patience, seat need, phase, empty held/consumed/order lists.
waiting Place order through world. If world rejects menu/stock, leave with nothing_appealing. active_order_id, order ids, orders placed, phase waiting.
seat attempt Low seat need skips tables. Medium/high claims first table with open capacity. High no-seat sensitivity may leave immediately if friction exceeds patience. table_id and table_claimed_at when seated.
order result Ready orders become delivered and held. Failed orders may create a replacement order if budget and max-order gates allow it. Held items, budget spent, received time, next reorder check, order_failed phase if needed.
consuming Every held item is consumed through world; drinks use sip_drink, food uses eat_item. Consumed item ids and consumption start time.
dwelling Pickup-only customers dwell for max 5 seconds. Others dwell for profile target; workers require a seat to dwell. Dwell start, recurring friction snapshots, possible reorder attempts.
done Leave satisfied, impatient, no seats, too expensive, nothing appealing, or cancelled. Left time, reason, released table, actual dwell seconds, unconsumed-item flag.

5. Reorder gates

Gate Must be true
Visit is open Customer is not done and has no active order.
Order cap remains orders_placed < max_orders_per_visit.
Budget remains budget_spent < budget and at least one affordable order candidate exists.
Time has arrived next_reorder_check_at exists and current time is past it.
Still patient Current friction does not exceed patience.
Chance roll passes Visit RNG roll is less than archetype reorder chance: commuter 0%, worker 45%, leisure 15%.

The world stays authoritative

Profiles describe demand. They do not directly mutate the cafe. The world accepts, rejects, records, and reports every operational action.

  • Orders and stock are owned by world methods, so sold-out and preparation failures stay consistent.
  • Tables and phases are synced into customer visits for dashboard visibility.
  • Metrics and reports capture spawns, lifecycle events, wait behavior, consumption, revenue, and archetype totals.
